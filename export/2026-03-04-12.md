## MIGHTEE/COSMOS-3D: The discovery of three spectroscopically confirmed radio-selected star-forming galaxies at z=4.9-5.6
- ArXiv: 2602.05808v2
- Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05808v2
- Updated (UTC): 2026-03-03T15:53:18+00:00
- Authors: R. G. Varadaraj, A. Saxena, S. Fakiolas, I. H. Whittam, M. J. Jarvis, R. A. Meyer, C. L. Hale, K. Kakiichi, M. Li, J. B. Champagne, B. Jin, Z. J. Li, M. Shuntov
- Abstract: Radio observations offer a dust-independent probe of star formation and active galactic nucleus (AGN) activity, but sufficiently deep data are required to access the crossover luminosity between these processes at high redshift ($z>4.5$). We present three spectroscopically confirmed high-redshift radio sources (HzRSs) detected at 1.3 GHz at $z=4.9$-$5.6$, with radio luminosities spanning $L_{\rm 1.3 \, GHz}\approx2$-$5\times10^{24} \, \rm W \, Hz^{-1}$. These sources were first identified as high-redshift candidates through spectral energy distribution (SED) fitting of archival Hubble, JWST NIRCam+MIRI, and ground-based photometry, and then spectroscopically confirmed via the $\rm H\,α$ emission line using wide-field slitless spectroscopy from JWST COSMOS-3D. The star formation rates (SFRs) measured from SED fitting, the $\rm H\,α$ flux, and the 1.3 GHz luminosity, span $\sim100$-$1800\, M_{\odot} \, \rm yr^{-1}$, demonstrating broad agreement between these SFR tracers. We find that these three sources lie either on or $0.5$-1.0 dex above the star-forming main sequence at $z=4$-6 and have undergone a recent burst of star formation. The sources have extended rest-UV/optical morphologies with no evidence for a dominant point source component, indicating that an AGN is unlikely to dominate their rest-UV and optical emission. Two of the sources have complex, multi-component rest-frame UV/optical morphologies, suggesting that their starbursts may be triggered by merging activity. These HzRSs open up a new window towards probing radio emission powered by star formation alone at $z> 4.5$, representing a remarkable opportunity to begin tracing star formation, independent of dust, in the early Universe.

射电观测提供了一个独立于尘埃的星形成与活动星系核（AGN）活动的探针，但要在高红移（z>4.5）范围内达到两者之间的临界发光强度，需要足够深的数据。我们报道了三个经光谱红移确认的高红移射电源（HzRSs），在1.3 GHz下检测到，红移为 z=4.9-5.6，射电光度范围约为 L_1.3GHz ≈ 2–5×10^24 W Hz^-1。这些源最初通过对档案中的 Hubble、JWST NIRCam+MIRI，以及地面观测的光谱能量分布（SED）拟合，被识别为高红移候选对象，随后通过 JWST COSMOS-3D 的宽场裂片光谱学检测到的 Hα 发射线进行了光谱红移确认。通过 SED 拟合得到的星形成率（SFR）、Hα 通量和 1.3 GHz 射电光度，覆盖约 100–1800 M⊙ yr^-1 的范围，显示出这些 Star-formation 率 指标之间的广泛一致性。我们发现这三源位于 z=4–6 的星形成主序线上，或高出 0.5–1.0 dex，并且最近经历了一次星形成爆发。它们在 rest-UV/光学波段具有扩展的形态，未见明显的点源主导证据，表明在其 rest-UV 与光学发射中，AGN 不太可能占主导地位。这些源中有两源在 rest-frame UV/光学上呈现复杂的多分量形态，提示其星爆可能由并合活动触发。这些 HzRSs 为仅由星形成驱动的射电发射在 z>4.5 时段开辟了新的窗口，为在早期宇宙中不依赖尘埃来追踪星形成提供了重要机会。

**Summary**
1. 研究背景
- 射电观测可以在不依赖尘埃的情况下同时探测星形成与 AGN 活动，是研究早期宇宙星-AGN 共演的重要工具。
- 要在高红移（z>4.5）达到星形成与 AGN 射电发射的分界，需要极深的射电数据与多波段证据以区分两者。
- 本研究通过深度多波段观测结合光谱确认，首次给出在 z≈5 附近的高红移射电源，揭示以星形成为驱动的射电辐射在这一时期的可观测性与特征。

2. 先前方法与局限性
- 先前对高红移星形成活跃系统的研究，往往依赖于光学/近红外的尘埃受遮蔽的星形成迹象，或对射电信号的星-AGN 区分能力不足。
- 缺乏大样本的光谱红移确认对象，难以明确区分射电源的驱动机制（星形成 vs AGN），以及在 z>4.5 时段的星形成强度与主序位置。
- 高红移 HzRS 的系统性发现和多 tracers 校验仍然稀缺，需要结合深度射电、红外/光学形貌及光谱观测。

3. 主要贡献
- 发现并通过光谱红移确认三颗 HzRS，在 1.3 GHz 的射电检测下，红移为 z=4.9–5.6，射电光度约为 L_1.3GHz ≈ 2–5×10^24 W Hz^-1。
- 使用 Hubble、JWST NIRCam+MIRI 与地面观测数据的 SED 拟合，初步识别高红移候选对象，随后通过 JWST COSMOS-3D 的 Hα 宽场裂片光谱学确认红移。
- 从 SED、Hα 与 1.3 GHz 射电信号中得到相互一致的星形成率（约 100–1800 M⊙ yr^-1），并发现这三源处于或高于星形成主序的区间（z=4–6），且近期有星形成爆发。
- 观测到的扩展型 rest-UV/光学形态与缺乏显著点源表明AGN并不主导其紫外/光学发射。两源的复杂多分量形态提示并合触发星爆的可能性。
- 为在 z>4.5 时段通过射电观测直接追踪星形成提供了新的窗口，展示了在早期宇宙中不依赖尘埃的星形成追踪的可行性与潜力。

4. 方法概览
- 数据与样本：使用档案 Hubble、JWST NIRCam+MIRI 与地面观测的多波段光强数据进行 SED 拟合，筛选高红移候选对象。
- 光谱确认：利用 JWST COSMOS-3D 的 wide-field slitless spectroscopy 测得 Hα 发射线，完成三源的光谱红移确认。
- 射电观测与星形成率推导：在 1.3 GHz 波段测量射电光度，结合 SED 与 Hα 的星形成率估计，比较三者的一致性。
- Morphology 与 AGN 评估：通过 rest-UV/光学形态分析，评估是否存在显著点源分量，进而判断 AGN 对发射的主导性；对两源进行多分量结构的解构以探讨并合触发情况。
- 结果解读：将结果放在 z=4–6 的星形成主序框架内，讨论其最近的星形成爆发及可能的触发机制。

5. 任务与性能
- 任务：在 z>4.5 区间发现并确认三颗高红移射电源，量化其星形成率、射电光度与形态特征，评估其驱动机制（星形成 vs AGN），并探索其在早期宇宙中通过射电手段追踪星形成的可行性。
- 性能指标与结果：三源的红移为 z=4.9–5.6，1.3 GHz 射电光度约 2–5×10^24 W Hz^-1；基于 SED、Hα 与射电推断的 SFR 区间约 100–1800 M⊙ yr^-1，且位置在 z=4–6 的星形成主序线上或略高（0.5–1.0 dex），表明最近经历星形成爆发。两源显示复杂多分量的 rest-frame UV/光学形态，提示并合触发。整体结论是，在 z>4.5 的时段，可以通过射电观测直接探测到仅由星形成驱动的发射，为早期宇宙中不依赖尘埃的星形成追踪提供了新的窗口和机会。

## MOSS II: Mid frequency radio catalog of Saraswati core region
- ArXiv: 2603.03090v1
- Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03090v1
- Updated (UTC): 2026-03-03T15:31:55+00:00
- Authors: Robert Kincaid, Edwin Retana-Montenegro, Bruno Slaus, Viral Parekh, Pascale Jablonka, Sameer Salunkhe, Shishir Sankhyayan, Vernesa Smolčic, Marco Bondi
- Abstract: The MeerKAT Observations of the Saraswati Supercluster (MOSS) is an ongoing project attempting to study the radio and optical properties of the core region of the Saraswati supercluster which will eventually entail a full survey of the entire supercluster region. We have used MeerKAT L-band (1.28 GHz) images at an angular resolution of 8 arcsec from previous deep (central RMS noise of 11 - 16 uJy beam-1) pilot observations of the core region (z ~ 0.28) of the Saraswati supercluster containing the two most massive galaxy clusters: Abell 2631 and ZwCl2341. These cluster fields cover an area of 1.6 deg2 and the radio catalogs produced from each cluster region contain 1999 and 2611 sources (5sigma limit) for Abell 2631 and ZwCL2341, respectively. For each catalogue, we investigated the noise properties, astrometry, flux density scale accuracy, spectral properties, etc of the radio sources. The catalogs were then corrected for various observational biases before derivation of the radio source counts. In agreement with previous studies, we find that at the sub-mJy level our counts show the characteristic flattening, indicating the increased dominance of the star-forming galaxy (SFG) population over the active galactic nuclei (AGN). Furthermore, in this sub-mJy regime the counts lie slightly higher (a 'bump' feature) compared to other deep MeerKAT data and recent radio-sky simulations. We suggest that this feature could be attributed to an enhanced population of intermediate SFG and/or AGNs associated with these galaxy cluster fields. In addition cosmic variance could represent an important source of uncertainty in the source counts.

## Little Red Dots as self-gravitating discs accreting on supermassive stars: Spectral appearance and formation pathway of the progenitors to direct collapse black holes
- ArXiv: 2507.22014v2
- Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2507.22014v2
- Updated (UTC): 2026-03-03T09:59:00+00:00
- Authors: Lorenz Zwick, Christopher Tiede, Lucio Mayer
- Abstract: We propose an alternative physical interpretation and formation pathway for the recently discovered "little red dots" (LRDs). We model LRDs as super-massive stars (SMSs) surrounded by massive self-gravitating accretion discs (SMDs) that form as a consequence of gas-rich major galaxy mergers. The model provides an excellent match for numerous spectral features of LRDs, where the V-shape arises from the superposition of two black bodies, and Balmer line broadening is sourced by the intrinsic rotation of the SMD. No additional AGN, stellar, dust, or broadening component is strictly required. This results in a model with physically motivated parameters that are robust to variations in observed LRD properties. We perform MCMC fits for two representative LRD spectra, for which the full parameter posterior distributions are determined. Allowing for a compressed SMS mass-radius relation, the recovered parameters are compatible with sub-Eddington accretion in self-gravitating discs, and the recovered SMS masses of few $ 10^6$ M$_{\odot}$ imply the subsequent formation of massive black holes (BH) that squarely follow the expected BH mass--galaxy mass relation, while also predicting a cut-off luminosity of order few $10^{44}$ erg/s in quantitative agreement with current observations. While matching the abundance of LRDs is challenging, the association to galaxy mergers produces a redshift distribution that reflects observations.

## Compact Absorbers surrounding the M87 AGN Revealed by ALMA Band 3 Observations1
- ArXiv: 2603.02759v1

- Updated (UTC): 2026-03-03T08:58:12+00:00
- Authors: Mahitosh Ray, Chorng-Yuan Hwang
- Abstract: We report detection of two compact absorption features surrounding the M87 nucleus and some extended absorption features perpendicular to the M87 jet using the Atacama Large millimeter-submillimeter-Array (ALMA) Band 3 data. One compact absorption feature appears at the position of the M87 AGN and the other $0.5\arcsec$ away from the AGN. These two compact features appear as well-defined negative structures in the integrated intensity map from the velocity range of $-500$ to $+2000~{\rm km~s^{-1}}$. - 参数化：所有模型参数直接绑定于喷流的物理条件，与传统单区模型的自由度数量相当但更具物理起源性。
- 数值求解：对沿喷流方向的辐射过程进行数值计算，覆盖质子（在内喷流达到 EeV 能量）与电子（在 pc 量级）的辐射贡献，得到中微子与多波段电磁谱的耦合预测。
- 天体应用与预测：对 TXS 0506+056 的多波段拟合与中微子谱预测，以及对 PKS 0605-085 的潜在关联性分析，给出对未来观测的明确预期。
- 物理含义：质子扩散特性为 $E^{0.3}$，对扩散机制的约束指向非 Bohm 型扩散；近 BLR 区的粒子注入对极端事件的再现性提供解释。

5. 任务与性能
- 任务：给出一个与喷流物理紧密耦合的 leptohadronic 框架，用以解释 blazar 的中微子输出并检验其能量预算的可行性，同时对多波段观测提供一致的解释与预测。
- 表现（结果与意义）
  - 对 TXS 0506+056 的应用：质子在 inner jet 被加速至 EeV，产生的中微子通量与 IceCube 的十年点源数据相吻合，且中微子谱可达到 ~100 PeV 的数量级；0.1 pc 处的质子辐射对应 X 射线与 γ 射线，pc 尺度的电子辐射对应光学数据。
  - 能量预算：质子功率约为 Eddington 光度的 ~1%，提升了模型的能量可行性与物理可信度。
  - 谱与扩散：粒子谱为 $E^{-1.8}$，扩散标度为 $E^{0.3}$，排除了 Bohm-like 扩散的简单假设。
  - 2017 年事件的解释性：在 BLR 附近的额外粒子注入能够再现与高能中微子相关的 2017 年伽玛事件。
  - PKS 0605-085：该源可能与 KM3NeT 最近探测到的 >100 PeV 中微子相关，需要进一步观测确认。
  - 总体意义：blazars 在 ultra-high-energy 领域是高效的中微子发射源，提升了它们成为未来针对这一能量区间进行探测的首要候选对象的可能性。

## Rapid jet ejection from PKS 0215+015 coincident with a high-energy neutrino event
- ArXiv: 2603.02325v1
- Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02325v1
- Updated (UTC): 2026-03-02T19:00:09+00:00
- Authors: F. Eppel, M. Kadler, E. Ros, P. Benke, L. C. Debbrecht, J. Eich, P. G. Edwards, M. Giroletti, A. Gokus, S. Hämmerich, J. Heßdörfer, M. Janssen, S. Kim, D. Kirchner, Y. Y. Kovalev, T. P. Krichbaum, R. Ojha, G. F. Paraschos, F. Rösch, W. Schulga, J. Sinapius, J. Stevens
- Abstract: Aims. We present a new neutrino-blazar multiwavelength flare coincidence observed in the blazar PKS 0215+015, which showed a strong multiwavelength outburst in coincidence with the IceCube neutrino track alert IC220225A, similar to the case of TXS 0506+056. We investigate the immediate response of the radio jet to the major flare. Methods. We performed target-of-opportunity observations with the Very Long Baseline Array (VLBA) at 15, 23, and 43 GHz in full polarization for six epochs with monthly cadence following the neutrino event. We combine the VLBA observations with monitoring data from the Effelsberg 100-m telescope, the Australia Telescope Compact Array, and Fermi/LAT. Results. Based on our VLBI kinematic analysis, we identified a new rapid jet component with an apparent speed of ~60-80c, which was ejected around the arrival of IC220225A. The fast component ejection is traced by a characteristic signature in polarization that suggests a shock-shock interaction with a quasi-stationary feature. By combining the VLBI results with radio variability data, we estimated a bulk Lorentz factor of $Γ= 105 \pm 56$ and a jet viewing angle of $\vartheta = (1.47 \pm 0.31)^\circ$. Conclusions. We note that the properties of the rapid component exceed previously reported maximum apparent jet speeds and Lorentz factors from continuous VLBI monitoring programs. This is likely only possible because we are observing an exceptional flaring event at high redshift (z=1.72) with higher observing cadence than in typical monitoring programs. We suggest that neutrino production in PKS 0215+015 can occur through pγ-interactions with protons possibly accelerated within the fast-moving feature. The target photon field could be external to the jet or explained by a multi-layered jet. The latter scenario is consistent with the presence of quasi-stationary features revealed in our analysis.
