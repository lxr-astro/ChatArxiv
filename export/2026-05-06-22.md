## A Multi-parameter Fuzzy Set Framework for Classifying Red, Blue, and Green Valley Galaxies
- ArXiv: 2605.04028v1
- Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04028v1
- Updated (UTC): 2026-05-05T17:51:14+00:00
- Authors: Amit Mondal, Biswajit Pandey
- Abstract: We present a data-driven fuzzy set framework for classifying galaxies into the red sequence, blue cloud, and green-valley populations using multiple observables from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS DR18). Unlike traditional methods based on hard boundaries in colour or stellar mass, our approach assigns continuous membership degrees using sigmoidal functions derived from bimodal galaxy properties, including $(u-r)$ colour, specific star formation rate (sSFR), and $D4000$. Membership functions are constructed via Gaussian mixture modeling and combined using a conservative fuzzy minimum operator. Applying this method to a volume-limited sample of 88,579 galaxies, we compare with the empirical classification of \citet{schawinski14}. The fuzzy approach reduces contamination in the red and green-valley populations and yields more physically consistent distributions of star formation and morphology. Red galaxies show a unimodal low-sSFR distribution, while green-valley galaxies exhibit clearer signatures of morphological evolution. We also examine the dependence of active galactic nucleus (AGN) fraction on stellar mass and find no significant differences between methods, indicating robust global AGN trends. However, clustering analysis reveals subtle differences: fuzzy-classified red galaxies show enhanced large-scale clustering, suggesting a stronger association with highly biased dark matter halos. These results demonstrate that fuzzy classification provides a flexible, physically motivated alternative to hard-cut methods, enabling a more accurate and interpretable view of galaxy populations and their evolution.

## Optical Variability Structure Function of Low-Luminosity AGN using ATLAS Lightcurves
- ArXiv: 2605.03577v1
- Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03577v1
- Updated (UTC): 2026-05-05T09:45:16+00:00
- Authors: Ashley Hai Tung Tan, Christian Wolf, Neelesh Amrutha, Christopher A. Onken, John L. Tonry, Rachel Webster
- Abstract: The origin of the optical flux variability in active galactic nuclei (AGN) is largely unknown. Previous studies have correlated features of the variability structure function (SF) with AGN properties, though they mostly involved high-luminosity AGN to avoid biases from host galaxy flux. In this work, we characterise optical variability in a sample of 246 low-luminosity AGN at $z < 0.1$ from the Six-degree Field Galaxy Survey (6dFGS) through the ensemble variability SF. We use lightcurves from the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS) with a cadence of $\sim$2 days over eight years, and perform host-AGN decomposition on recent spectra to obtain the host fraction. We find that the slope of the SF depends on black hole mass, increasing from $\sim 0.1$ at $\log M_{\mathrm{BH}}/M_\odot \sim 6.5$ to $\sim 0.3$ at $\log M_{\mathrm{BH}}/M_\odot \sim 8$. Contrary to some earlier work, We do not find breaks in the SF, and two-epoch spectra taken 20 years apart suggest that the SF keeps rising into decadal timescales. In addition, we measure an anticorrelation of the amplitude with the luminosity and a positive correlation with the black hole mass. The variability behaviour also suggests that extinction is not the main driver of the variety in Seyfert subtypes.

**Translated Abstract**
活动星系核（AGN）的光学通量变异起源在很大程度上尚不清楚。以往的研究将变异结构函数（SF）的某些特征与AGN属性相关联，但它们大多涉及高光度AGN，以避免宿主星光引入的偏差。本研究通过6dFGS中的246个低光度AGN（红移在 z < 0.1）样本，利用集合变异函数（ensemble SF）来表征光学变异性。我们使用ATLAS的光变曲线，观测节律约为每日两次，覆盖约8年，并在最近的光谱中对宿主星系与AGN进行分解以获得宿主分量比例。结果显示，SF的斜率与黑洞质量相关：在 log(M_BH/M_sun) ≈ 6.5 时约为0.1，增至 log(M_BH/M_sun) ≈ 8 时约为0.3。与部分早期工作不同，我们没有发现SF中的断点；利用相隔约20年的两次光谱观测表明，SF在十年尺度内仍在上升。此外，我们测得SF振幅与光度呈负相关、与黑洞质量呈正相关。变异行为还提示，消光并非驱动Seyfert亚型差异的主要因素。

## Variable ADAF disk as the origin of Changing-Look AGN
- ArXiv: 2603.28666v2
- Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28666v2
- Updated (UTC): 2026-05-05T00:37:07+00:00
- Authors: Chun Xu
- Abstract: We propose that changing-look AGN transitions arise from variations in the size of the inner ADAF disk. The AGN accretion disk consists of an outer thin disk and an inner thick ADAF component, whose size is intrinsically unstable and evolves over time. The size variations of the ADAF are governed by a parameter η, which represents the turbulence strength within the accretion flow. ηalso determines the accretion rate onto the central black hole and controls jet formation and outflow rate, with the latter regulating the line-of-sight absorption. From the perspective of a variable ADAF, changing-state and changing-observation AGN are two sides of the same coin. We further discuss gigahertz-peaked and compact steep-spectrum radio sources as possible manifestations of intermediate-to-large scale ADAFs. Finally, we propose that AGN unification models should include both orientation and ADAF size as key parameters.

**Translated Abstract**
我们提出，Changing-Look（变化型）AGN的转变源于内层 ADAF 磁盘尺寸的变化。AGN 的吸积盘由外部薄盘和内部厚的 ADAF 构成，其尺寸具有内在的不稳定性并随时间演化。ADAF 尺寸的变化由参数 η 控制，η 表示吸积流中的湍动强度。η 亦决定落在中心黑洞的吸积率，并控制喷流的形成与外流速率，而后者又调控沿视线的吸收。以变量 ADAF 的视角看待，changing-state 与 changing-observation 的 AGN 是同一现象的两面。我们进一步讨论 gigahertz-peaked 与 compact steep-spectrum 射电源作为中等到大尺度 ADAF 的可能表现。最后，我们提出 AGN 统一模型应将取向与 ADAF 尺寸作为关键参数共同纳入。

**Summary**
1. Research background
- 改变型（Changing-Look, CL）AGN 展示出显著的谱型转变，常被视为取向、吸积速率波动或遮挡结构变化等因素的综合结果。该工作从吸积盘的结构出发，提出内层 ADAF（Advection-Dominated Accretion Flow）尺寸的时间演化，可以自然解释 CL-AGN 的状态切换及观测变化，且与黑洞喷流和沿视线吸收之间的联系相关联，为理解 CL-AGN 提供一个统一的物理框架。

2. Prior methods and limitations
- 以往的解释往往聚焦于（1）沿视线的可变遮挡（如云团或尘扇遮挡）导致的观测差异，或（2）吸积速率的改变引发的谱型变化，然而难以同时解释与喷流/外流相关的物理量变化以及与无线电观测的联系（如射电源的形态与谱型）。此外，缺乏将内部 ADAF 尺寸的动态稳定性及湍动强度纳入统一框架的尝试，从而难以给出统一的 CL-AGN 预测。

3. Main contributions
- 提出将 CL-AGN 的转变归因于内层 ADAF 尺寸的变化：外部薄盘 + 内部厚 ADAF 的结构组成，以及 ADAF 尺寸的内在不稳定性与时间演化。  
- 引入参数 η，作为吸积流中的湍动强度，既控制 ADAF 的尺寸演化，也决定落在黑洞的吸积率，并影响喷流形成和外流速率，进而调控沿视线的吸收。  
- 从“变量 ADAF”的角度指出 changing-state 与 changing-observation 的 AGN 实质上是同一物理过程的不同观测角度。  
- 将 GHz-peaked 与 compact steep-spectrum 射电源视为中到大尺度 ADAF 的可能表现，为射电源谱与结构的统一解释提供新的线索。  
- 提出 AGN 统一模型应同时把取向与 ADAF 尺寸作为关键参数纳入。

4. Method overview
- 模型框架：把 AGN 吸积盘分解为外部薄盘与内部 ADAF，研究 ADAF 尺寸随时间的演化及其驱动因子。  
- 控制参数：引入 η 来表征湍动强度，直接影响 ADAF 的尺寸、黑洞吸积率、喷流/外流强度以及沿视线的吸收。  
- 可观测连接：从 ADAF 尺寸的变化出发，解释 CL-AGN 的光谱型转变、吸收特征以及相关的射电源类型（GHz-peaked 与 compact steep-spectrum）的可能对应关系。  
- 预测与检验路径：提出若干可观测的相关性与时间尺度，如吸收变化率、喷流强度与射电形态随 ADAF 尺寸的变化而同步演化，进而通过多波段观测进行检验。

5. Task and performance
- Task（任务）：提供一个统一的物理框架来解释 Changing-Look 与 Changing-Observation 两种表现形式的 AGN，并解释与喷流、吸收及射电源类型之间的联系。  
- Performance（表现/预测能力）：该模型在理论上自然将 CL-AGN 与射电源谱型的多样性联系起来，给出可测试的预测，如 ADAF 尺寸与吸收、喷流、射电谱形态之间的相关性，以及随时间的同步演化。  
- 未来工作与验证要点：需要通过时间序列多波段观测（X-ray/光学/射电）来追踪 ADAF 尺寸的演化与观测量的变化，并结合射电源形态与谱型的变化来检验 η 的预测关系；同时应将 orientation 与 ADAF 尺寸一起纳入 AGN 统一模型的对比检验中。

## ArkenstoneBH. A model for high-specific energy black hole feedback in cosmological simulations
- ArXiv: 2605.03154v1
- Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03154v1
- Updated (UTC): 2026-05-04T20:51:10+00:00
- Authors: James M. Sullivan, Greg L. Bryan, Matthew C. Smith, Jake S. Bennett, Drummond B. Fielding, Bryan A. Terrazas, Sophie Koudmani, Rachel S. Somerville, Michaela Hirschmann
- Abstract: AGN feedback is a key piece of galaxy evolution but is difficult to model due to its high specific energies, multiphase nature, and limited simulation resolutions. Arkenstone is a subgrid framework for representing multiphase flows in coarse resolution simulations that has been used to model stellar feedback driven galactic winds. It ensures the correct treatment of high specific energy feedback that would otherwise be challenging to model accurately in Lagrangian simulations. We introduce the new Arkenstone BH model, which extends the Arkenstone framework to model black hole feedback. We focus on describing the first piece of this framework, which follows the hot, high specific energy phase of these outflows. The second piece, which treats their multiphase structure with a scheme for modeling unresolved cold clouds, will be implemented and described in a later paper. We present Arkenstone BH in simulations of an isolated galaxy to demonstrate the framework and its ability to capture high specific energy feedback that interacts only weakly with cold, dense gas. We show how these energetic outflows suppress star formation in our isolated galaxy by counteracting the inflow of gas from the circumgalactic medium into the interstellar medium. This work is part of the "Learning the Universe" collaboration, which aims to understand the Universe's underlying physics and initial conditions.

**Translated Abstract**

## Wave interference as the origin of the cyclic magnetorotational dynamo in accretion disks: insights from weakly nonlinear theory and local shearing box simulations

- Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02996v1

The emf depends on the mean field $\mathbf{B}$ more generally than standard mean-field closures allow.
